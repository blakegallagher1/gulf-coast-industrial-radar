# Project Context

Gulf Coast Industrial Radar is an AI-agent platform concept for detecting industrial project formation before public bid day or broad market awareness.

The idea came from a broader Ideabrowser exploration of high-potential, lower-competition business ideas and then narrowed into Baton Rouge / Gulf Coast heavy industrial work: chemical plants, refineries, LNG, data centers, ports, power, pipelines, and large industrial construction.

## Current First-Pass Thesis

The first buyer is **real estate investors and developers**.

The platform should detect industrial demand and site-control formation early enough for investors/developers to:

- Buy land.
- Option land.
- Entitle land.
- Sell into industrial demand.
- Avoid land where risks or false-positive signals outweigh opportunity.

## Hard Constraint

Use **free/public data sources only** for the first pass.

Excluded from the first pass:

- Paid parcel aggregators.
- Paid deed pulls.
- GovWin/GovTribe subscriptions.
- Paid satellite feeds.
- Proprietary bid-alert databases.

## Later Buyer Segment

Contractors and vendors remain a strong later market, but they are not the first-pass buyer. Their workflow becomes relevant after project formation is detected and stage labels become useful for prequalification, EPC tracking, bid monitoring, and outreach.
