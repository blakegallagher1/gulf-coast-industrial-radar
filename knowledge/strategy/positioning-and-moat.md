# Positioning And Moat

## Positioning

Gulf Coast Industrial Radar is a pre-announcement industrial formation intelligence product for real estate investors and developers.

The product is strongest when it answers:

- Where is industrial demand forming?
- Which sites or corridors are quietly becoming more valuable?
- Which entities may be assembling land?
- Which public signals corroborate the thesis?
- What should an investor/developer do next?

## Moat

The moat is not generic AI. The moat is the cumulative local signal graph:

- Free-source coverage by parish/county and source type.
- Entity alias history.
- Parcel and site history.
- Sponsor and facility aliases.
- Industrial infrastructure proximity features.
- Prior project timelines.
- Source-specific extraction rules.
- Feedback on which signals produced real investment opportunity.

## Why Free Sources Matter

Starting with free sources forces the product to build durable source coverage, extraction logic, and confidence scoring before relying on paid data. It also keeps the first-pass workflow reproducible and easier to validate.

## Differentiation From Existing CRE Tools

The April 30, 2026 research frames the market gap as project-formation detection, not ordinary property lookup.

| Existing workflow | Gap |
|---|---|
| CoStar, Altus/RCA, Reonomy, LandVision, LightBox, and broker networks | Strong at historical data, parcel/property lookup, or market intelligence, but not specifically positioned around Gulf Coast industrial pre-announcement alerts from public signals |
| Manual analyst monitoring | Can catch signals, but is slow, fragmented, and hard to scale across parish/county, permit, utility, agenda, and filing systems |
| Generic AI real estate tools | Broad interest in AI, but limited Gulf Coast industrial depth and weak source provenance |

The defensible wedge is a local signal graph with source provenance, backtested lead time, and investor/developer action logic.

## Current Moat Hypotheses

- First regional evidence library for Gulf Coast industrial formation signals.
- Source-specific adapters and extraction rules that survive messy public systems.
- Backtest dataset showing which source combinations predicted real projects early.
- Analyst feedback loop distinguishing valid formation from ordinary land transactions.
- Local entity and alias graph for sponsors, shell LLCs, registered agents, law firms, ports, utilities, and industrial corridors.
